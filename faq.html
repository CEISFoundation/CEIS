FAQ & About the CEIS

What is the CEIS?

The Community Ran Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences (CEIS) is a collaborative catalog of integer sequences, inspired by the OEIS. It serves mathematicians, researchers, educators, and enthusiasts by offering a shared repository of structured data, commentary, and references.

How are sequence numbers assigned?

Sequences receive identifiers of the form A000000. When you submit a new sequence, the system determines the next available identifier and assigns it automatically. This ensures a consistent, chronological catalog.

What are the submission requirements?
  • Provide at least four integer terms, separated by commas.
  • Use a descriptive title that reflects the sequence’s defining property.
  • Include any known formulas, references, or proofs in the comments.
  • Ensure the data is accurate; cite sources when appropriate.
Is attribution mandatory?

Attribution is optional. If you leave the author field blank, the sequence will be listed as originating from “Anonymous”.

How do I explore number theory topics?

Use keywords in your searches—terms like “multiplicative”, “partition”, “recurrence”, “Catalan”, or “modular” will surface related sequences. Comments and descriptions are indexed, enabling discovery through contextual phrases, generating functions, and cross-references.
